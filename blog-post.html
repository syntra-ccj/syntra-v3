Why Point Solutions Are Killing Your Logistics Operation

Tive for tracking. Sensitech for temperature. Samsara for fleet. And none of them talk to each other. Here's the real cost of a fragmented stack — and what to do about it.

The Stack Nobody Planned For

Every tool in your logistics stack started as a solution to a real problem. Temperature excursion on a pharma shipment? Add Sensitech. Fleet going dark on cross-border hauls? Add Samsara. Customer asking for real-time tracking? Add Tive.

Each decision made sense in isolation. But at some point, you looked up and realized you were running five platforms, none of them talking to each other — and your team was spending more time switching between dashboards than actually executing.

This is the point solution trap. And it's costing you more than you think.

The hard truth: The average logistics operation uses 4–7 separate software tools for tracking, fleet management, warehouse ops, and customer visibility. Almost none of them share data natively.

The Real Cost of Fragmentation

The cost isn't just the SaaS subscription fees (though those add up). The real cost is operational:

  • Decision latency. When a temperature alert fires in one system and your TMS is in another, someone has to manually bridge the gap. Every minute that takes is a minute your cargo is at risk.
  • Data silos. Your IoT devices know things your ERP doesn't. Your WMS has no visibility into what's happening on the road. The data exists — it just can't talk to itself.
  • Duplicate work. How many times does a shipment get entered into multiple systems? How many manual status updates does your team send that a connected platform would automate?
  • Accountability gaps. When something goes wrong, nobody can reconstruct the full chain of events because the data lives in four different platforms with four different timestamps.

Average reduction in operational overhead when logistics companies move from fragmented point solutions to a unified execution platform. The labor savings alone typically justify the switch within the first quarter.

Visibility Is Not the Same as Execution

Here's something the visibility-first vendors won't tell you: knowing where your shipment is doesn't help you do anything about it.

A dot on a map is not a decision. A temperature alert is not a response. Real-time data is worthless if it can't trigger an action — a route change, a carrier notification, a chain-of-custody record, a customer update — without someone manually connecting the dots.

This is the difference between a visibility tool and an execution platform. Visibility tells you something happened. Execution does something about it.

What a Connected Stack Actually Looks Like

A true logistics execution platform doesn't just aggregate data — it creates an operational layer that sits between your systems and your team. When a WaypointXL fires a geofence alert, the platform can:

  • Cross-reference that event against the TMS expected arrival window
  • Trigger a customer notification if the delay exceeds threshold
  • Log the event in the chain-of-custody record automatically
  • Flag the carrier in the ERP for performance tracking

All of that happens in seconds, without a human in the loop. That's not visibility. That's execution.
